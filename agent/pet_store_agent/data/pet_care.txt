PET CARE GUIDE

DOG CARE
========

Chihuahua Care Guide
-------------------
Chihuahuas are small but spirited dogs that require special attention due to their size.

Bathing:
- Frequency: Every 3-4 weeks or when dirty
- Use lukewarm water (not hot or cold)
- Use dog-specific, gentle shampoo
- Be extra gentle due to their small size
- Dry thoroughly after bathing to prevent chills
- Consider using a low-heat blow dryer on cool setting
- Regular brushing between baths helps maintain coat health

Feeding:
- Small, frequent meals (2-3 times daily)
- High-quality small-breed formula
- Avoid overfeeding (prone to obesity)
- Fresh water always available
- Monitor for hypoglycemia in puppies

Exercise:
- Short walks (15-20 minutes) twice daily
- Indoor play is often sufficient
- Avoid extreme temperatures
- Use harness instead of collar

General Dog Hydration
--------------------
Proper hydration is crucial for all dogs' health and wellbeing.

Water Requirements:
- General rule: 1 ounce per pound of body weight daily
- Increase during hot weather or after exercise
- Fresh, clean water available 24/7
- Change water at least once daily
- Use stainless steel or ceramic bowls

During Walks:
- Bring portable water bottle with bowl attachment
- Offer water every 15-20 minutes in hot weather
- Watch for signs of dehydration:
  * Excessive panting
  * Dry nose and gums
  * Lethargy
  * Loss of skin elasticity
- Rest in shade during hot days

Puppy Training Basics
--------------------
Start training early for best results.

House Training:
- Establish routine immediately
- Take outside every 2 hours for young puppies
- Praise and reward successful outdoor elimination
- Never punish accidents
- Use enzymatic cleaner for accidents
- Consistency is key

Basic Commands:
1. Sit: Hold treat above nose, move back over head
2. Stay: Start with short durations, gradually increase
3. Come: Use happy voice, never call for punishment
4. Down: Lure from sit position with treat
5. Leave it: Essential for safety

Socialization:
- Critical period: 3-14 weeks
- Expose to various people, dogs, environments
- Keep experiences positive
- Puppy classes recommended
- Continue throughout first year

CAT CARE
========

Indoor Cat Enrichment
--------------------
Keeping indoor cats mentally and physically stimulated.

Environmental Enrichment:
- Multiple vertical spaces (cat trees, shelves)
- Window perches for bird watching
- Rotating toys to maintain interest
- Scratching posts in multiple locations
- Hidden treats or puzzle feeders

Play Activities:
- Interactive play 15-20 minutes twice daily
- Feather wands for hunting simulation
- Laser pointers (always end with physical toy)
- Catnip toys (for cats that respond)
- Paper bags and boxes for exploration

Mental Stimulation:
- Food puzzles and slow feeders
- Clicker training for tricks
- Cat TV (videos for cats)
- Grow cat grass indoors
- Rotate toy availability

Litter Box Management
--------------------
Proper litter box care ensures cat happiness and house cleanliness.

Setup Guidelines:
- Number of boxes: # of cats + 1
- Size: 1.5x length of cat
- Location: Quiet, accessible areas
- Avoid: Near food/water, high-traffic areas
- One box per floor in multi-story homes

Maintenance:
- Scoop daily (twice for multiple cats)
- Complete litter change weekly
- Wash box with mild soap monthly
- Replace plastic boxes yearly
- Use unscented, clumping litter

Problem Prevention:
- Keep consistently clean
- Don't change litter type suddenly
- Ensure easy access for senior cats
- Address medical issues promptly
- Reduce stress in multi-cat homes

Senior Cat Care
--------------
Special considerations for cats over 7 years old.

Health Monitoring:
- Bi-annual vet checkups
- Watch for weight changes
- Monitor water consumption
- Check for dental issues
- Note behavior changes

Comfort Modifications:
- Lower-sided litter boxes
- Ramps to favorite spots
- Softer bedding for joints
- Heated beds in winter
- Non-slip food mats

Nutrition:
- Senior-formula food
- Smaller, frequent meals
- Monitor kidney function
- Ensure adequate hydration
- Consider supplements (vet-approved)

SMALL PET CARE
==============

Hamster Housing
--------------
Creating the ideal hamster habitat.

Cage Requirements:
- Minimum 450 square inches floor space
- Bar spacing under 0.5 inches
- Solid floor (not wire)
- Good ventilation
- Secure latches

Essential Setup:
- 6+ inches bedding depth for burrowing
- Exercise wheel (solid surface, 8"+ diameter)
- Hide houses (multiple)
- Water bottle (checked daily)
- Food bowl (heavy ceramic)

Environmental Needs:
- Temperature: 65-75Â°F
- Away from direct sunlight
- Quiet location
- No drafts
- Consistent day/night cycle

Guinea Pig Social Needs
----------------------
Guinea pigs are highly social animals.

Companionship:
- Should be kept in pairs minimum
- Same-sex pairs or neutered groups
- Introduce gradually
- Provide multiple resources
- Monitor for compatibility

Space Requirements:
- Minimum 10.5 sq ft for 2 pigs
- Additional 2-4 sq ft per extra pig
- Horizontal space more important than vertical
- Multiple hiding spots
- Separate feeding areas

Daily Interaction:
- Handle gently daily
- Talk softly during approach
- Support body fully when holding
- Floor time for exercise
- Positive reinforcement training

BIRD CARE
=========

Parrot Enrichment
----------------
Keeping intelligent parrots engaged and happy.

Mental Stimulation:
- Rotate toys weekly
- Foraging opportunities
- Puzzle feeders
- Training sessions daily
- Social interaction 2+ hours daily

Physical Exercise:
- Out-of-cage time supervised
- Flight harness for outdoor time
- Climbing structures
- Various perch sizes
- Wing exercises

Environmental Setup:
- Natural wood perches
- Variety of textures
- Toys at different heights
- Quiet retreat area
- Natural light exposure

Finch Care Basics
----------------
Care requirements for small songbirds.

Housing:
- Horizontal flight space priority
- Minimum 30" wide for pairs
- Multiple perches at varying heights
- Natural branches preferred
- Nest boxes for breeding pairs

Diet:
- Quality finch seed mix base
- Fresh vegetables daily
- Egg food during breeding
- Cuttlebone for calcium
- Clean water changed daily

Social Needs:
- Keep in pairs or groups
- Same species compatibility
- Adequate space prevents aggression
- Multiple feeding stations
- Visual barriers in cage
